EP_01: THE OUTSIDER

● SEASON 1

Rue Asfaw reunites with her childhood friend, James Hawthorne, after years of silence. They start by revisiting shared memories, but Rue quickly realizes James is hiding the true nature of their trip. The mystery begins with a quiet, human lie.

Transcript for Episode 1: The Outsider

[Train Station Ambience / Chatter]

Mother: Pierre, how could we forget him? What if something happens?

Father: Marie, calm down. He’s old enough to handle himself. He’s probably enjoying the peace and quiet.

Mother: He’s twelve, Pierre! What if he needs us? What if he doesn’t answer?

Father: (Teasing) He's fine!

Mother: This is serious!

Father: Do you remember that movie Home Alone, right? Maybe he's living his best version of it.

Mother: We're not in a movie, Pierre. Stop joking.

Father: Relax, Marie. He is smarter than we were at his age.

PA System: Attention, please. The train departing for Mount Étoile is now boarding. First stop: Arbor's Crossing. Please board immediately.

Rue: It's a busy evening.

Traveler: I don't know why I thought coming here would fix anything. It doesn't feel right.

Teen: No, dude, I swear it was real! Like, it was just hovering above the field. Not a plane, not a drone—just there.

Friend: Right. And next you'll say aliens left you a message.

PA System: Next stop: Arbor's Crossing. For those continuing to the mountain lodges, your stop will follow shortly. Thank you.

Traveler with Map: This trail isn't even on the map. How am I supposed to find it if it doesn't exist?

Rue: Isaiah would have found something poetic to say about this. Probably something like... "Trains don't care how empty they are... they just keep moving."

Rue: I better text him when I get to James's. He'll worry if I don't.

PA System: Next stop: Crestfall Valley. Most passengers for the lodges will depart here. Thank full for traveling with us.

[ A 2-minute section of credits and ambiance follows before the story resumes. ]

Rue: Sorry about that, it's September 22nd.

Rue: Still no signal... just trees.

Rue: It's strange... seeing him again after all this time. Why now?

Rue: It's been about three hours since the train left. Starting to feel... anxious.

Conductor: Billet, s'il vous plaît.

Rue: Sorry?

Conductor: Your ticket, please.

Rue: One second... Here it is.

Conductor: Yeah... Just not a fan of these parts. You're not from around here, are you?

Rue: Just passing through.

Conductor: Be careful. People get lost out here.

Rue: That was weird... his hands were shaking.

PA Announcement: Prochain arrêt: Saint-Faustin-Lac-Carré. Veuillez noter que la correspondance pour Mont-Tremblant arrive bientôt.

[Train stops]

Rue: Arrived at the station. James is already here...

James: Still the early bird, huh?

Rue: You know me better than that.

Rue: This was your dad's, right?

James: Yeah. It's weird, but it's mine now. Had to. We've got gear and supplies for the trip.

Rue: Supplies? What kind of trip is this?

James: I'll explain everything when we're all together.

Rue: We're? There's more people? Long. Quiet. No signal the whole way. So... why now? I mean... it's been a while, James.

James: It's just... a lot has happened.

Rue: Yeah, but I feel like I'm missing something.

James: It's a long story.

Rue: You've always been bad at asking for help. Whatever's going on... it feels big, James. Bigger than you're letting on.

James: It's not something I can explain yet. Hey, you'll see soon enough. Once we get there.

Rue: James picked me up... Apparently there are more people. Are you narrating this trip or something? Something like that. I thought it'd be nice to keep track. You know... for myself and all your friends.

James: That's perfect! That's exactly what we're doing too—documenting everything. We've got cameras, audio equipment, the whole setup. We're planning to bring it all back and piece the story together.

Rue: Wait, I was joking... You sound pretty serious.

[ The conversation continues until the end of the episode. ]
